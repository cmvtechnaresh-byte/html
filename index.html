Expert Guidance

Connectivity Tips for Hajj & Umrah Pilgrims

Advice from experienced pilgrims and our support team to keep you connected throughout your journey.

📅

Install 1–2 Weeks Before Departure

Installing at home on your own Wi-Fi is stress-free. The data plan only activates when you arrive in Saudi Arabia, so installing early wastes nothing. Trying to install at Jeddah airport is risky — eSIM cannot be downloaded at the destination.

📶

Enable "Data Roaming" for the eSIM Line

This is the single most common activation issue. After installing, go to Settings → Cellular/Mobile → your Konttigo eSIM → turn Data Roaming ON. Saudi Arabia networks require this setting to connect.

🔋

Bring a Power Bank for Mina Days

During the 3–4 days in Mina, access to charging points is limited in the tents. A 20,000mAh power bank ensures your phone stays charged — and your eSIM connection stays active — throughout the Hajj days.

📲

Pre-Download Maps & Apps Offline

Download offline Google Maps for Makkah, Madinah, and Mina before you travel. Even with a great eSIM, network congestion during peak prayer times (e.g., Fajr at the Haram) can slow speeds briefly.

👨‍👩‍👧

Use Hotspot for Your Group

If some family members have devices that don't support eSIM (older phones), enable your Konttigo eSIM hotspot. All Konttigo plans include unlimited hotspot sharing so the whole family stays connected.

🔧

Troubleshooting: Toggle Airplane Mode

If your phone shows "No Service" after landing, toggle Airplane Mode ON for 30 seconds, then OFF. Wait 2–3 minutes. This resolves 70% of activation issues. Still stuck? Our 24/7 support team is one chat message away.

An eSIM (embedded SIM) is a digital SIM card built into your smartphone. For Saudi Arabia, your Konttigo eSIM connects to local networks (STC, Network, Zain), giving you 4G/LTE/5G data coverage across 66 cities — without needing a physical SIM, fingerprint scan, or local registration.
